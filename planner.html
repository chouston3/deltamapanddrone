How the score works. Each daylight hour inside your solar-noon window is graded on six axes weighted for multispectral capture. Wind (25%): wind is read at 120 m (≈390 ft, your flight altitude), not the ground, since the air is faster aloft. Under 6 m/s ideal; 8–10 caution; over 12 m/s or gusts over 12 = no-go. Ground-only forecasts under-read the wind your drone fights. Gusts at altitude are estimated from the ground gust scaled by the wind shear, so treat them as modeled, not measured. Visibility: under 3 statute miles is a hard no-go (Part 107 minimum); 3–5 mi flags as hazy. Cloud (20%): clear or uniform overcast score well; partly cloudy (≈30–70%) is penalized because drifting light wrecks radiometric consistency. Precip (20%): rain or ≥60% chance = no-go. Canopy (17%): modeled from hours since last rain plus temp/dewpoint spread, wind, and solar radiation — a wet canopy is a hard no-go because surface water suppresses NIR and skews NDVI/NDRE; "drying" caps the hour at caution. Sun angle (12%): target ≥ 40° for stable corn/soybean reflectance; 30–40° caution; hours outside the noon window are excluded outright. Temp (5%): outside −10–40 °C is out of spec. Field access is an advisory from trailing 48-hour rainfall — Delta clay holds water, so treat it as a heads-up, not a gate. Mission load assumes a calibratable acres-per-battery (default 150 ac at ~400 ft AGL multispectral mapping). Weather: Open-Meteo. Sun position and solar noon: NOAA algorithm, computed locally. None of this replaces a field check or an airspace check.
