What it is

Same idea as a medical X-ray. The imagery shows where the turf is stressed and exactly where to look. Your superintendent or grounds crew reads the cause and writes the prescription. I fly, process, and hand you a clear picture. You make the call.

What every flight delivers

See the whole field at once

A high-resolution aerial image of the entire field, stitched together, so you can assess conditions and plan work without walking every yard.

Catch declining areas early

NDVI and NDRE turf-health mapping can reveal areas of declining vigor before they are obvious to the eye, giving you lead time to investigate and act before a problem spreads.

Stay ahead of wear and safety

Goalmouths, sidelines, and high-traffic zones mapped and located, so you can address thinning and uneven areas before they affect play or safety.

Spend your budget where it counts

Uneven-coverage and stress patterns flagged and located, so inputs and labor go to the areas that need them instead of the whole field.

Know exactly where to look

Stress and problem patches marked on the map, pointing your crew straight to the spots worth a closer look on the ground.

A record you can hand up the chain

A plain-language written summary plus secure digital delivery, so you have clear documentation for your own planning or to show a director, committee, or budget holder.

What I do

Turf-health imaging is the heart of it: I read what the turf is telling you and hand it back flagged and located, so your crew knows exactly where to look and what to act on.

It is not all I do. Want a full orthophoto of the grounds, or a 3D model of the clubhouse? Just ask. If it can be flown, let's talk about it.
